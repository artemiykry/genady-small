# People

People are first-class entities so colleagues can be referenced, queried, and reasoned about — not just typed as strings.

## What a person is

- **Name** and any aliases ("Alex" / "Alexandra Chen")
- **Role** — free text (title, function)
- **Teams** — one or more [teams](teams.md) they belong to
- **Contact** — a map of identifiers (`email`, `slack`, `github`, `phone`, …). Open-ended; add what you need.
- **Status** — `active`, `former` (left / no longer relevant)

## Where they come from

- Manually created
- Auto-suggested from calendar attendees (you approve before they become entities)

## What you can do

- `@mention` them anywhere — tasks, notes, docs, meetings, incidents
- Filter any task view by person
- Ask the [agent](../agent/overview.md): *"what did Alex and I last discuss?"*

## Profile

Each person's page has three sections.

### Overview

The at-a-glance view: role, teams, contact, recent meetings together, open tasks involving them, recent mentions across notes and docs.

### One-on-ones

Every 1:1 [meeting](meetings.md) with this person, oldest to newest. Each is a regular meeting note; they collect here automatically. Scroll back through what you've discussed over months.

### Brief

A single [document](documents.md) attached to the person — topics to raise next time, longer-term things to work on together, feedback to share, items to follow up on. Survives across one-on-ones. Pull items from here into the next 1:1; check them off as they resolve.

## What lives elsewhere

- Meeting attendance → [meetings](meetings.md)
- Assignment of work → [tasks](tasks.md)
