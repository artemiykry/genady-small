# Meetings

A meeting is a note anchored to a calendar event. It's where you put what was discussed, decided, and what to do next.

## Lifecycle

- **Before** — the meeting note auto-exists for any upcoming event. You can open it and pre-fill agenda items.
- **During** — quick capture: bullets, decisions, action items. Mention [people](people.md) with `@`, link other entities with `[[ ]]`.
- **After** — action items become [tasks](tasks.md) with one keystroke. Decisions are searchable forever.

## Structure (suggested, not enforced)

- **Attendees** — pulled from the event, linked to person entities
- **Agenda** — what we want to cover
- **Notes** — free-form
- **Decisions** — explicit, marked, surfaced in search
- **Action items** — convertible to tasks, with assignee and due date

## Anchoring

- One meeting note per event. Recurring events share a series; each occurrence has its own note, but the series is browsable as a whole.
- An ad-hoc meeting (no calendar event) can be created as a standalone meeting note and optionally attached to an event later.

## What you can do

- Jump from a calendar event to its note (and back).
- See all meetings with a person on their profile.
- See all meetings linked to a document, project task, or incident in one place.
- Ask the [agent](../agent/overview.md) to draft a recap, extract action items, or summarize a series.
